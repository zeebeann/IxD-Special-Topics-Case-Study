Zainab Ilyas

Introduction

Throughout this course, I have explored the intersection of 3D motion graphics and projection mapping, focusing on how these techniques can be applied in retail environments to create engaging, immersive experiences. My research has involved experimenting with After Effects’ 3D motion graphic capabilities, studying projection mapping techniques, and analyzing real-world applications such as Nike and other retail installations. This process has allowed me to develop a deeper understanding of how motion graphics can enhance physical spaces and communicate ideas effectively. By combining layered animations, dynamic transitions, and spatial storytelling, I have been able to create visuals that not only capture attention but also guide viewers through a cohesive narrative. Overall, this work demonstrates the potential of motion graphics to transform environments and enhance user experiences through thoughtful integration of digital and physical design elements.

Module 2: Prototype

Research

Project 2 explores the integration of 3D motion graphics and projection mapping to create a retail-focused installation that enhances the presentation of a physical product. Using After Effects as the primary tool, the process began with experimenting in 3D space and expanded into researching projection mapping techniques and real-world retail applications. Through this exploration, the project evolved from a broad investigation of motion graphics into a more focused approach that considers spatial context, scale, and how visuals interact with a physical object. The selection of AirPods Pro as the final product reflects a balance between creative potential and technical feasibility. Its simple form, consistent design, and strong visual identity allow for the integration of effects such as sound-waves, lighting, halation, and typography without overwhelming the product itself. The design emphasizes clarity and cohesion, ensuring that motion enhances the product experience rather than competing with it. The final prototype is a storyboard prototype that maps a complete projection mapping visualization intended to be displayed above or beside the physical product. Each scene is designed with spatial awareness, guiding viewer attention through controlled motion, intentional transitions, and a consistent visual language. Overall, this project demonstrates how motion graphics can extend beyond the screen to create immersive, engaging experiences that connect digital visuals with real-world environments.

Research Summation

The research process for this project involved a combination of experimentation and analysis. Initially, I explored the capabilities of After Effects for creating 3D motion graphics, which provided a foundation for understanding how to manipulate digital elements in a spatial context. This experimentation was complemented by research into projection mapping techniques, which informed how to effectively integrate motion graphics with physical environments. Analyzing real-world applications, such as Nike’s retail installations, offered insights into how motion graphics can enhance product presentation and engage audiences. Throughout the process, I developed a cohesive concept that balances creative expression with technical feasibility. The iterative nature of the research allowed me to refine my approach, ensuring that the final prototype effectively communicates the intended narrative while maintaining visual appeal and spatial coherence.

Reflection

This project explored the use of 3D motion graphics and projection mapping as a spatial design approach within a retail context, combining experimentation in After Effects with research into real-world projection installations and product advertising. Through iterative testing and action research, I developed my ideas by constantly refining both visual and technical elements, particularly when initial effects such as the halo concept did not work as intended and had to be reworked into more effective solutions like particle-based systems. Early projection testing was a key learning moment, highlighting the importance of designing for physical environments where lighting, contrast, and viewer perspective directly impact visibility and experience. Research into retail projection mapping and motion design references helped shape my visual direction, while exploring tools such as 3D modelling workflows and Eyecandy techniques supported the development of a cohesive visual language. Overall, the project strengthened my ability to balance creative ambition with technical constraints and deepened my understanding of motion graphics as a medium that can extend beyond the screen into immersive, spatial experiences.
